The 20 Best Kids' Movies Hitting Theaters in 2019

So many movies, so little time.

By Carson Blackwelder
Dec 6, 2018
Source

With 2018 winding down, it’s time to look ahead at what next year will bring to the theaters. We’ve compiled a list of the best kids’ movies to keep an eye out for in 2019. The whole family will want to see these brand-new, animated and live-action films (yes, including you, parents!). Click through and plan accordingly!

1 of 20
Columbia Pictures; Bona Film; Group Pariah
A Dog's Way Home

BUY TICKETS

Release date: January 11, 2019

A pup named Bella (voiced by Bryce Dallas Howard) bravely embarks on a 400-mile journey home in the film adaptation of W. Bruce Cameron's 2017 best-selling novel of the same name. Prepare for some Homeward Bound-like feels.

2 of 20
20th Century Fox; Working Title Films; Big Talk Productions
The Kid Who Would Be King

BUY TICKETS

Release date: January 25, 2019

A bullied middle schooler (Louis Ashbourne Serkis) pulls King Arthur's famous sword Excalibur from stone and, while forming a new version of the Round Table, must face medieval villainess Morgana—with the help of the magical Merlin.

3 of 20
Warner Animation Group; Animal Logic; Vertigo Entertainment; Rideback
The LEGO Movie 2: The Second Part

BUY TICKETS

Release date: February 8, 2019

A direct sequel to the 2014 original, we return to Bricksburg only to find that everything is a little less awesome because of some out-of-this-world invaders. It’s up to Emmet (Chris Pratt) and some LEGO pals to save the day.

4 of 20
20th Century Fox; Lightstorm Entertainment; Troublemaker Studios
Alita: Battle Angel

BUY TICKETS

Release date: February 14, 2019

A big-eyed cyborg (Rosa Salazar) sets out to find the truth about who she really is and to find her place in the futuristic world. This movie is based on Yukito Kishiro's similarly titled manga and has been a long time coming.

5 of 20
DreamWorks Animation
How to Train Your Dragon: The Hidden World

BUY TICKETS

Release date: February 22, 2019

For the third and final installment in this franchise, we rejoin Hiccup (Jay Baruchel)—who is now chief—and Toothless—who has discovered an untamed mate. The two must leave their home and travel to a hidden world to save everything they love.

6 of 20
Marvel Studios
Captain Marvel

BUY TICKETS

Release date: March 8, 2019

Before Avengers 4, get ready for another superhero origin story as we explore what made Carol Danvers (Brie Larson) the titular Captain Marvel—all while the Earth is caught in the middle of an intergalactic war between two alien races.

7 of 20
Paramount Animation; Nickelodeon Movies; Ilion Animation Studios
Wonder Park

BUY TICKETS

Release date: March 15, 2019

As a child, June dreamt up an amusement park named Wonderland filled with all kinds of crazy rides and talking creatures. Having grown up a bit, June has lost that imaginative spirit—until she finds that same theme park for real.

8 of 20
Walt Disney Pictures; Tim Burton Productions; Infinite Detective Productions; Secret Machine Entertainment
Dumbo

BUY TICKETS

Release date: March 29, 2019

Nearly 80 years after the animated Disney classic about an elephant with ears so big he is capable of flying, we get a live-action version directed by Tim Burton and featuring a cast including Eva Green, Colin Farrell, and Michael Keaton.

9 of 20
DC Films; New Line Cinema; The Safran Company; Mad Ghost Productions
Shazam!

BUY TICKETS

Premiere date: April 5, 2019

In the latest DC film, we follow Billy Batson (Asher Angel), a teenager who can transform into an adult superhero (Zachary Levi) just by uttering one magic word: "Shazam!" Rest assured, this won’t be your typical comic book movie.

10 of 20
Laika
Missing Link

BUY TICKETS

Premiere date: April 12, 2019

Myths and monsters investigator Sir Lionel Frost (Hugh Jackman) and free-spirited adventurer Adelina Fortnight (Zoe Saldana) set out to prove the existence of a legendary creature named Mr. Link (Zach Galifianakis), who just wants to find some long-lost relatives.

11 of 20
Warner Bros. Pictures; Legendary Pictures; The Pokémon Company
Pokémon: Detective Pikachu

BUY TICKETS

Release date: May 10, 2019

The first-ever live-action Pokémon movie focuses on a Pikachu (voiced by Ryan Reynolds), everyone's favorite electric mouse, who really wants to be a detective. This LOL-worthy film has a cast including Justice Smith and Ken Watanabe, to name a few.

12 of 20
STX Family; Troublemaker Studios; Original Force
UglyDolls

BUY TICKETS

Release date: May 10, 2019

Who's ready for another jukebox musical comedy based on a toy fad, specifically the plush UglyDolls? Whether you are or you aren't, one is coming and it features A-list voices including Kelly Clarkson and Nick Jonas.

13 of 20
Walt Disney Pictures; Rideback; Marc Platt Productions
Aladdin

BUY TICKETS

Release date: May 24, 2019

Journey back to Agrabah in this live-action retelling of the 1992 Disney classic and get reintroduced to Aladdin (Mena Massoud), Jasmine (Naomi Scott), and Genie (Will Smith). And of course, the villainous Jafar (Marwan Kenzari) will also return to ruin the day yet again.

14 of 20
Marvel Entertainment; TSG Entertainment; The Donners' Company; Kinberg Genre
Dark Phoenix

BUY TICKETS

Release date: June 7, 2019

Familiar faces return to the big screen for the latest X-Men installment. We'll follow Jean Grey (Sophie Turner) as she begins to develop remarkable—though uncontrollable—powers after being hit by a solar flare that turns her into the Dark Phoenix.

15 of 20
Universal Pictures; Illumination
The Secret Life of Pets 2

BUY TICKETS

Release date: June 7, 2019

Little is known about the sequel to its 2016 predecessor, but we can likely expect more shenanigans from Max (voiced by Patton Oswalt) and the rest of the furry crew—including Tiffany Haddish, Nick Kroll, and more—while their owners aren't around.

16 of 20
Walt Disney Pictures; Pixar Animation Studios
Toy Story 4

BUY TICKETS

Release date: June 21, 2019

After being passed down from Andy to Bonnie, our favorite group of toys—including Woody (Tom Hanks) and Buzz Lightyear (Tim Allen)—meet a fellow toy named Forky and go on an adventure with friends new and old. Bring tissues!

17 of 20
Columbia Pictures; Marvel Studios
Spider-Man: Far From Home

Release date: July 5, 2019

Following Avengers 4, we'll be back with Peter Parker (Tom Holland) who—along with friends—is trying to enjoy summer vacation in Europe only to be rudely interrupted by a villain named Mysterio (Jake Gyllenhaal). Curse you, spidey senses.

18 of 20
Walt Disney Pictures; Fairview Entertainment
The Lion King

BUY TICKETS

Release date: July 19, 2019

The 1994 Disney classic gets a CGI remake this year with Donald Glover as our hero, Simba. Also in the all-star cast are Seth Rogen, Chiwetel Ejiofor, Billy Eichner, John Oliver, Keegan-Michael Key, James Earl Jones, and—of course—Beyoncé.

19 of 20
Paramount Players; Nickelodeon Movies; Walden Media
Dora the Explorer

Release date: August 2, 2019

Isabela Moner will play the beloved Nickelodeon character who explored the jungles for much of her young life, Dora. She comes to life just in time to tackle her newest challenge: high school. Yes, Boots and Diego will be there, too!

20 of 20
20th Century Fox; Genre Films; Sunswept Entertainment
The New Mutants

BUY TICKETS

Release date: August 2, 2019

If you haven’t gotten your X-Men fill yet, we've got another one for you. The New Mutants follows five young mutants who are being held in a secret facility against their will after having discovered their abilities. Will they escape and save themselves? Time will tell.
